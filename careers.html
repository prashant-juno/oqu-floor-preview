Ethos

How the floor thinks

Four beliefs that shape what gets built and what gets thrown away.

Ownership over outputs

You own the result end to end, not the handoff. If a deal stalls on your desk at 11 p.m., you care that it stalls.

Bias for clarity and simplicity

Plain answers, small systems, fewer moving parts. If it needs a diagram to excuse it, it is too clever.

Respect for regulated industries

Lending is regulated for good reasons. We build the audit trail first and the autonomy second.

Long-term thinking

We would rather compound quietly than sprint loudly. Decisions are made for year five, not week five.

AI Security Engineer

Agent securityRemote or LondonAlways open
Read the briefClose the brief

The agents on this floor read documents from strangers, move money data between regulated systems and, on autopilot, sign underwriting decisions inside the client's credit policy. Your desk exists to make sure nothing else can. You would own the security of the agent layer end to end: prompt injection and jailbreak resistance on hostile documents, permissioning between agents and the systems they touch, audit-trail integrity, and red-teaming the autopilot before anything ships.

You have broken or defended production LLM systems. You can think like a fraudster reading a loan file and like an auditor reading your logs. You write code and threat models with equal comfort. Lending experience is welcome; paranoia is essential.

This desk stays open for exceptional people rather than for a headcount plan. If the brief reads like your diary, we want to hear from you.
